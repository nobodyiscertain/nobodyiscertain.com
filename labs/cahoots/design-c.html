Chapter 01

The empty office

There used to be a moment. You'd walk into the office, coffee in hand, and just... know. Who was in. Who was heads-down. Who was free to talk.

It wasn't a feature. Nobody built it. It was just the natural byproduct of sharing a space.

Remote work deleted that moment.

Now you send Slack messages into the void. You schedule 30-minute meetings for 2-minute questions. You don't know if someone's deep in flow or scrolling Twitter — so you default to async, and the conversation that would have taken 90 seconds takes a day.

Chapter 02

What we actually lost

Not the commute. Not the fluorescent lights. Not the sad desk lunch.

We lost the ambient awareness of working alongside other humans. The ability to glance up and see that your teammate is at their desk. The spontaneous "hey, got a sec?" that led to the best ideas. The feeling that you're building something together, even on a quiet Tuesday.

Video calls don't replace this. Slack doesn't replace this. Virtual offices with pixel avatars definitely don't replace this.

Chapter 03

What if you could bring it back?

Not the office. Just the awareness. The presence. The ability to see who's around and connect in one click.
